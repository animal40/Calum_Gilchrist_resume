About me

After completing my BSc in Architecture, following in the footsteps of my grandfather, I made the decision that this was not the career path I wished to pursue.

From here I made the move into Education. Inspired by my favourite teachers from school and a belief I could pass on my knowledge and skills with the same enthusiasm that they showed me, I undertook my Post Graduate degree in Secondary Education (Design & Technology). I have enjoyed my career in education and have added to and built upon my skillset over the past 10 years in this career:

  • Communication
  • Leadership
  • Planning and Organisation
  • Presentation skills
  • Problem Solving
  • Resourcefulness
  • Working in a Team
  • Working Independently

I have reached a stage in my Education career where I don't feel as challenged as I would like to and do not see a progression route which I wish to pursue.

This is why I have undertaken a Software Engineering Bootcamp; to set myself a new challenge and push myself to become stimulated by my work as I am in other aspects of my life; family and hobbies. I have chosen to pursue Software Engineering as coding and putting together my own websites when I was younger was something I really enjoyed doing, and after undertaking a 5 day coding challenge my mind was made up. I applied to Hyperion Dev, who are linked with University of Edinburgh, and I was able to apply for and secure funding for the course through the Scottish Funding Council. I am loving the course and how much it challenges me and also how much I have learned within a short space of time.

On the bootcamp I have learned:

  • how to build applications as a software engineer
  • about Object Oriented Programming and agile development
  • programing languages: Java and Python

It has been a challenge to balance the course, a full-time job, family life and hobbies, but one I have relished as my goal is to better my life by changing career.
